# Stage Call Feel Pass Ideation
Date: 2026-02-24  
Direction: Festival Poster Energy  
Scope: Ideation only (no implementation in this document)

## Creative North Star
Stage Call should feel like stepping into a live festival campaign poster that is moving, reacting, and getting louder as your run builds momentum. The player should sense:
- urgency without panic,
- celebration without clutter,
- style with readable gameplay.

The visual identity should prioritize bold editorial composition over “mobile game generic.” Every screen should communicate the same statement: this is a high-energy, city-scale music event.

## Visual System
### Typography
- Display: tall condensed uppercase for major moments (menu title, round complete, combo milestones).
- Functional: clean geometric sans for numbers, timers, and controls.
- Contrast principle: large dramatic headings + compact tactical data.

### Color
- Base world: muted green + concrete gray to keep routes legible.
- Event accents: orange, cyan, lime as stage identities.
- State accents:
  - success: mint/gold sparks,
  - warning: amber,
  - fail pressure: warm red-orange (not deep red).

### Shape Language
- Rounded-rectangle chips + ticket-like panels.
- Poster-edge overlays and diagonal separators in UI to imply printed collateral.
- Large clean silhouettes for stage/distraction elements.

## Interaction Feel
### Motion Grammar
- Entrance: staggered rise + slight scale-in for menu overlays.
- In-round feedback: quick kinetic pops, not floaty drifts.
- Completion moments: one strong “hero” beat (fanfare + burst + summary reveal).

### Tactile Feedback
- Path draw should feel “inked” into a poster map.
- Hazard interactions should read as social friction (thought bubbles, interruptions), not punishment overlays.
- Combo chain should feel like crowd momentum building.

## HUD + Information Tone
- Keep HUD compact and punchy: score, level, encounters, timer as chips.
- Replace neutral labels with event-like phrasing where helpful:
  - “Set Score,” “Crowd Pressure,” “Time to Slot.”
- On mobile, reserve central upper area for ephemeral callouts and keep persistent stats thumb-safe/readable.

## Audio Identity
### Music Arc
- Chill (early): daytime optimism.
- Energy (mid): denser rhythm and movement.
- Peak (late): high-drive, late-night headliner intensity.

### SFX Character
- Prioritize recognizable identity over realism.
- Layered “festival crowd + synth design” hybrid.
- Avoid harsh transients for repeated effects; keep sessions fatigue-resistant.

## Emotional Beat Map
1. Start Screen: “You are producing a major event.”
2. Early Round: confident setup.
3. Mid Round: escalating pressure and flow.
4. Hazard Chaos: playful interruption, not frustration wall.
5. Round End: strong emotional punctuation (win/fail clarity).

## Design Risks to Watch
- Too much style can reduce readability under pressure.
- Over-animated overlays can block path planning.
- Excess audio brightness can cause fatigue in longer sessions.

## Suggested Next Validation Pass
1. Build two lightweight style mock variants under this direction:
   - A: cleaner editorial (more negative space),
   - B: maximal poster collage (more texture and overlays).
2. Run 5-minute play sessions and score:
   - readability under stress,
   - emotional engagement,
   - replay motivation.
